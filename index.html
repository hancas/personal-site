Push, Often.

Below are select samples of recent works. Each one was made with the intent of pushing creative boundaries and learning along the way. I'm always pushing code or pixels, often.

For more interactive features, please view this website on desktop devices.
2/8

haenyo

haenyo is a colorful Korean pop-up food venture started by Irvin Seo and Collin Morstein. We developed the haenyo identity to be a flexible visual system so that we could incorporate some of the flavor of the restaurants that graciously hosted us while still maintaining a recognizable presence. The pop-up has executed over 50 dinners since its inception in 2017.

3/8

Pilon

As a headless API-first e-commerce product, it was important that Pilon’s microsite appealed to advanced developers in the field. With this in mind, the design needed to be based around interactive visual elements, like the animated gradient background, to emphasize lightweight technology that’s easy to use. Take a look at pilon.io

4/8

ScratchMade

Collin Morstein and Irvin Seo expanded their skills in the kitchen beyond their pop-up dinners by creating a meal service with a plethora of options, all made from scratch. We created an identity and design system that drew inspiration from antique Italian and Russian recipe cards. We set out to create something playful and injected with some humor. Currently designing an online ordering system to be released in the fall of 2018.

5/8

Noona's

Noona is the Korean word for “older sister” and a references a special relationship between siblings and food. The illustration and handwritten script typography evoke fun and light-hearted childhood memories of pizza. Full designed menu and opening poster coming soon. Grand opening winter 2018.

6/8

Urban Teachers

For this project, I built all of the the front-end development with the goal of maintaining the integrity of the original flats and assets (designed by Christopher) This website was built from scratch, utilizing relative units instead of solid pixels, with CSS grids to keep the site light and responsive to emphasize the clean design.

7/8

Foodshed

Foodshed (founded by chef Spike Gjerde) is a Baltimore based restaurant group, consisting of: Artifact Coffee, Woodberry Kitchen, Parts & Labor, Grand Cru, and Bird in Hand. I worked in-house and directly with chefs and managers to design all material for events, menus, social presence, and retail packing within individual restaurant brand guidelines.

Small Projects

Smaller freelance illustration and design projects have often been the start of relationships that turn into the most fulfilling projects I’ve had the pleasure of working on.

I’m a Latinx multidisciplinary designer and illustrator currently living and working out of Baltimore, MD. My current obsession has been learning to build with code. I'm originally from Southern California and moved to Baltimore to attend MICA, where I graduated in 2015. I currently work at Kapowza as an Interactive Designer.

When I’m not working, I stay busy by reading physics articles, powerlifting, and cooking Ecuadorian food. My ideal weekend is eating a huge bowl of Phở and watching Curb Your Enthusiasm.
